Steak Marinade

Description

A steak marinade is a blend of acidic and aromatic ingredients, such as vinegar, citrus juice, garlic, herbs, and spices. The acid tenderizes the meat while the aromatics infuse it with flavor. Soy sauce, olive oil, and sweeteners like brown sugar or honey are common additions.

The marinade is applied to the steak, left to soak for 30 minutes to hours, and when grilled or seared, it enhances the steak's natural flavor, resulting in a tender, flavorful centerpiece for your meal.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup olive oil
  • ⅓ cup soy sauce
  • ⅓ cup fresh lemon juice
  • ¼ cup Worcestershire sauce
  • 3 tablespoons dried basil
  • 1 ½ tablespoons garlic powder
  • 1 ½ tablespoons dried parsley flakes
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried minced garlic (Optional)
  • ¼ teaspoon hot pepper sauce (Optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Steak: Ensure your steak is clean and trimmed of excess fat. Place it in a resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish that's large enough to accommodate the steak and the marinade.
  2. Pour the Marinade: Carefully pour the blended marinade over the steak, making sure it is evenly coated on all sides. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap.
  3. Marinating Time: Refrigerate the steak in the marinade for at least 30 minutes, but for optimal flavor infusion, marinate it for up to 4 hours or even overnight. If marinating for an extended period, turn the steak occasionally to ensure even distribution of the flavors.
  4. Preheat the Grill or Pan: When you're ready to cook, preheat your grill or a heavy-bottomed skillet over medium-high heat. Make sure it's hot before adding the steak.
  5. Cook the Steak: Remove the steak from the marinade, allowing any excess to drip off. Grill or sear the steak to your preferred level of doneness. Cooking times will vary depending on the thickness of the steak and your desired doneness, but a good rule of thumb is 4-6 minutes per side for medium-rare, adjusting as needed.
  6. Rest and Slice: After cooking, let the steak rest for a few minutes on a cutting board. This allows the juices to redistribute and ensures a juicy result. Then, slice the steak against the grain to maximize tenderness.
  7. Serve: Plate the sliced steak and serve it with your favorite side dishes, such as roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a fresh salad. Drizzle any remaining marinade over the steak slices for an extra burst of flavor.
